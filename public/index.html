About the Bot score — how it's calculated

The score is a heuristic from 0–100 (lower = more human-like) derived from the maker's last ~150 OpenSea bidding events. It's rate-based — we look at how fast and how widely a wallet is bidding, not just how many bids it has placed. Treat it as a yellow flag, not a verdict.

0–39 likely human 40–69 suspicious 70–100 bot-like

Signals we weight

The mini bar under each score shows these six segments left-to-right; segment fill goes green→red as that signal contributes more of its maximum.

General bot fingerprints (industry context)

  • Short-duration offers/listings (10–30 min): programmatic bidders use very short expirations so they can react to price moves without leaving open exposure.
  • High cancel-and-replace frequency: re-pricing every time the floor or top bid moves.
  • Offer-only behavior, no purchases: the goal is to be the highest bid at the moment an inattentive seller clicks "Accept", not to actually collect.
  • Mass-bidding across unrelated collections: a single wallet sending collection offers to dozens of unrelated projects.
  • Newly-created wallets with little history other than offers.
  • Round-number bids just below floor: heuristic-driven, not collector-driven.
  • No social presence: no ENS, no profile, no NFTs actually held in wallet.

Caveats

  • We look at the wallet's last ~150 OpenSea events (3 pages of 50). For an extremely active bot this may only cover a few minutes — which is fine, because the rate signal scales with the window automatically.
  • Edge case: a wallet that is both a real collector and bot-like (e.g. Dan4Play) can still score yellow/red. Use the Normies held / Burns columns as counter-signals — a wallet that holds or has burned Normies is much more likely to be a genuine community member regardless of score.
  • Normies held = current count of Normies in the wallet (from api.normies.art/holders/<addr>). Burns = total Normies the wallet has burned through the Canvas burn-to-edit mechanic (from /history/burns/address/<addr>, summed across commitments). Both serve as counter-signals — a wallet that holds or has burned Normies is much more likely to be a real collector regardless of score.
  • All 10 displayed offers are enriched. Subject to OpenSea's and api.normies.art's rate limits (60 req/min); repeat fetches of the same token reuse cached metadata.
